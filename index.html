My Rosemary, I’ve been wanting to put this into words in a way that feels as true as you are, so here it is, straight from my heart. Somehow, without forcing anything, you became my favorite person. You made love feel safe. You made commitment feel like peace, not a prison. You turned my “I’m not ready” into “I choose you.” And now look at us, happily married, building a life that still shocks me in the best way. I still remember when we were planning our wedding and I was lowkey scared. Not because I didn’t love you, but because I knew marriage is real… and you were too precious to mess it up. But that wedding day? Rosemary… that day is a memory I’d replay a thousand times if life gave me a remote control. Seeing you, standing there, knowing you were choosing me, my heart has never been so full. I felt like the luckiest man alive. And then came Oluwasikemi. I’ll never forget when we found out you were pregnant. It was honestly a mix of joy and fear, good news and “oh Lord, how will I do this?” Because at that moment, I didn’t feel financially ready to be a father. But even then, I was happy… because it was you. Because it was us. And today, when I look at our pretty daughter, I don’t just see a child, I see God’s blessing wrapped in a smile. Oluwasikemi is proof that grace can meet you right where you are and still lift you higher. Rosemary, thank you for believing in us. Thank you for staying with me long enough for love to grow past the beginning. Thank you for being my wife, my partner, my peace, my biggest gift. And let me say this clearly: I’m still choosing you. Not because I “have” to, but because I want you. I want your laughter, your heart, your presence, your love. You’re the best thing that has ever happened to me, and I don’t say that lightly. If I could go back to the day I met you, I would tap that old version of me on the forehead and say, “My guy… behave. That’s your wife.” I love you deeply, Rosemary. And I’m grateful for you every day, in every season. Always yours, Dr.Marv
